About Me

A longer introduction to my academic background, creative interests, technical direction, and portfolio goals.

I am a student at Sungkyunkwan University studying Film & Multimedia and Software. My academic path is built around the connection between creative production and technical execution. I am interested in the moment when visual ideas become working systems: a storyboard becomes an edited video, a design sketch becomes an interface, a course project becomes a web page, and a media concept becomes an interactive experience that people can actually use.


In Film & Multimedia, I have studied the foundations of cinematography, animation, editing, digital design, interface design, moving image, sound, short-form content, transmedia storytelling, film history, and art-related data analysis. These areas trained me to think about rhythm, framing, visual hierarchy, audience attention, narrative structure, and the emotional effect of media. I do not see media only as decoration; I see it as a structured communication system.


In Software, I have studied core topics such as systems programming, data structures, computer architecture, logic circuits, databases, operating systems, programming languages, and web programming. These subjects help me understand how digital products are built beneath the surface. They also help me approach creative work with a stronger sense of organization, maintainability, data flow, and computational logic.

My strongest interest is in hybrid projects where media production and software development support each other. For example, I am interested in portfolio websites that feel like curated exhibitions, interactive interfaces for digital artwork, AI-assisted tools for editing and content generation, media databases that make visual assets easier to manage, and Unity-based experiences that combine game logic with visual storytelling.


I enjoy working across multiple stages of production. I can plan the concept, organize references, build a visual system, edit images or videos, design the user experience, and then implement the structure with HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Python, databases, or other tools. This broad process is important to me because a strong digital project usually needs both creative sensitivity and technical responsibility.


Through this portfolio, I want to present myself as a creator who can communicate with designers, developers, editors, and planners. My goal is to keep building work that is visually clear, technically practical, and meaningful for users. Rather than separating art and engineering, I want to use both as parts of one production process.

Why Film & Multimedia Matters

Film and multimedia study helps me analyze how visual information is built. It teaches me to consider camera movement, editing rhythm, sound, composition, narrative flow, and audience experience. Why Software Matters

Software study gives structure to creative ideas. It allows me to build web pages, organize databases, understand how systems run, and create prototypes that can be tested. Instead of stopping at a visual concept, I can turn ideas into working digital experiences.
